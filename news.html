Uganda Joins the modern technology world after launching its first and only satellite

Uganda’s first ever satellite PearlAfricaSat-1 was successfully launched into the space on 17th November,2022 following it’s construction by three Ugandan aerospace engineers in collaboration with Kyushu institute of technology in japan under a multinational satellite design programme a broad a NASA spaceship

The satellite launch project first announced in 2019 took a major step forward together with a ground station in Mukono district,the Mpoma satellite .The station now serves a operating and communication centre for PearlAfricaSat-1 satellite was deployed into the low earth orbit to provide research and observation data will provide solutions weather forecasting ,agriculture monitoring,land,waters and mineral mapping among others.

As a country , the development of the PearlAfricaSat-1 present opportunities for Uganda.s participation in space science,meaning our engineers and scientist will be providing practical solutions to challenges facing Ugandan citizens as well as boosting the country’s internal capacity to develop the space and technology industry value chain .
